Impaired Water Quality: Lakes
Water quality directly influences the health of plant and animal communities. While many headwater wilderness watersheds have good water quality, degradation from historical activities such as mining or from upstream developments outside a wilderness may impact water quality in wilderness. This measure assesses number of lakes (or miles of streams) inside wilderness with impaired water quality, based on national or state 303(d) lists of impaired water bodies. When available, local monitoring data may also inform this measure.
